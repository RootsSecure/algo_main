# Dispatch SOP

## When to dispatch

Dispatch should be considered when:

- an incident is verified, and
- remote visibility is insufficient, or
- the owner explicitly requests on-ground confirmation, or
- the event suggests physical tampering, occupation, or construction activity

## Dispatch steps

1. Verify incident context.
2. Select partner aligned to property geography.
3. Create dispatch with ETA.
4. Ensure partner receives location and brief.
5. Track arrival.
6. Require closure notes and proof references.
7. Close the incident only after structured partner feedback.

## Closure expectations

Accepted closure outcomes include:

- site secure
- false alarm
- tampering confirmed
- trespass confirmed
- unauthorized construction observed
- device issue confirmed

## Data capture requirements

- ETA if known
- Arrival timestamp when reached
- Closure notes
- Proof references such as photos or short videos
