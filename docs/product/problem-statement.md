# Problem Statement

## Core problem

NRIs who own vacant land plots in India cannot reliably secure or monitor those plots remotely. Traditional property management firms typically focus on rent collection, caretaking, or periodic inspection, but they usually do not provide a continuous smart-security layer with cameras, tamper detection, incident verification, or immediate escalation.

## Symptoms experienced by owners

- Constant anxiety during long absences
- Dependence on neighbors or relatives for ad hoc updates
- No trusted proof of when a breach, encroachment, or construction attempt started
- Delayed response because suspicious activity is discovered too late
- Confusion between harmless motion and real site risk
- Silent monitoring failure when power, network, or batteries degrade

## Security scenarios the product targets

- Unauthorized entry onto the plot
- Illegal dumping or material storage
- Squatter occupation attempts
- Unauthorized construction activity
- Boundary-wall or gate tampering
- Long periods of camera or sensor downtime that create blind spots

## Why existing alternatives fall short

- Manual caretaking is inconsistent
- Generic CCTV solutions lack structured escalation and health monitoring
- Smart-home products assume an indoor structure and home Wi-Fi
- Property managers may inspect periodically but do not operate a live incident workflow

## Desired outcome

The owner should be able to open one system and see:

- Whether the plot is currently covered
- Whether any devices are offline or degraded
- What alerts happened and how severe they are
- Whether an incident was verified, escalated, or dismissed
- What evidence and field proof were captured
